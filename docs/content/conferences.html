Emotions 2023

Symposium
Tilburg, Netherlands
September 2023
Download Slides

I presented at the Emotions Conference in Tilburg, Netherlands, as part of a symposium titled Re-Examining the Facial Feedback Hypothesis: Investigating Emotion Induction through Functional Neuromuscular Electrical Stimulation (fNMES) of Facial Muscles. My talk highlighted empirical findings on how fNMES-induced facial expressions, such as smiling and frowning, influence emotion perception and emotional states.

Second Bodily and Cognitive Selves Eastern Arc Workshop

Poster Presentation
Essex University, Colchester, UK
13 June
Download Poster

I presented a poster at the Second Bodily and Cognitive Selves Eastern Arc Workshop, held at Essex University in Colchester, UK. The workshop brought together researchers from Essex, UEA, and Kent to explore interdisciplinary perspectives on bodily and cognitive selves. My poster focused on the interplay between facial neuromuscular electrical stimulation (fNMES) and emotional perception, highlighting preliminary findings on user experiences and the potential cognitive and affective implications of this novel technique.

This presentation contributed to discussions on innovative methods for studying embodied cognition and emotions within the context of interdisciplinary research.

Visit the workshop website for more information.

British Neuroscience Association (BNA) 2021

Symposium
Virtual Conference
April 2021
Download Poster

I participated in the British Neuroscience Association (BNA) 2021 virtual conference, where I presented research on participant concerns regarding the prospect of receiving facial neuromuscular electrical stimulation (fNMES). The talk explored user perspectives, highlighting apprehensions about safety, comfort, and the psychological impact of fNMES. This research aimed to inform ethical and user-centred approaches for future applications of fNMES in both experimental and clinical settings.

Visit the conference website for more information.
